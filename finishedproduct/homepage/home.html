My Thoughts on the OnePlus One

August 30, 2014, 12:00PM

  This phone is currently one of the most sought after phone for tech enthusiasts, as the "Never Settle" hype of offering premium specs for half of the competitor's price, coupled with the elusive invite system, has taken the tech world for a spin. After finally getting my hands on the Sandstone Black 64GB OnePlus One, these are some of my thoughts after 2 weeks of use.
  Starting with the initial design, the OnePlus One feels very sleek for $349. Although it has larger bezels than the Galaxy S5, I'd argue that the sleek design, where the display appears to float above the phone itself, looks much better than the bandage design of the Galaxy S5. It boasts capacitive touch buttons, which are a little bit dim in my unit. I would have preferred it if it was touch buttons from the OS instead of dedicated hardware keys. The power buttons are clicky. The back of the OnePlus One has the "Sandstone" back, which feels almost velvety, like alcantara, which is cool. Overall, amazing build quality compared to its Android competitors for $349.
